U DESIGN JUSTICE INITIATIVE

Workshop #1

Co-Create the Framework: Establishing the Foundation

Date: Tuesday, June 24, 2025
Location: City Building Design Lab (CBDL), Calgary

Workshop #1 launched the UDesign Justice Initiative by bringing together community members, students, and professionals to co-create the foundation for a community-led approach to design justice. The session focused on naming the initiative, identifying core values and principles, and establishing long-term priorities.

Key themes included addressing root causes in design education, centering community voices in planning and pedagogy, and building relationships that move beyond tokenism. Participants emphasized the importance of funding models rooted in lived experience, outreach to younger generations, and research that informs practice.

Ideas such as an annual conference, inclusive community hubs, and cross-disciplinary allyships emerged as actionable directions. The group committed to reframing success metrics, practicing active listening, and building spaces for care, equity, and knowledge-sharing.

This session laid the foundation for a new kind of design space that listens deeply, redistributes power, and builds from community-led visioning.

Workshop #2

Co-Decide the Framework: Building a Community-Led Structure for Design Justice

Date: Wednesday, August 14, 2025
Location: City Building Design Lab (CBDL), Calgary + Zoom

In Workshop #2, the UDesign Justice Initiative brings together community members, students, professionals, and partners to collectively shape the next phase of this work. We’ll build on insights from Workshop #1 to co-develop the structure, values, and strategies guiding the initiative.

This session will focus on:

  • Outlining funding pathways
  • Identifying meaningful partnerships
  • Setting clear, actionable next steps

More than a planning session, this workshop is a space to redistribute power, honour community expertise, and ensure that the direction of the initiative is shaped by those most impacted. Your voice is critical in creating a design process that is rooted in care, collaboration, and justice.

Whether you're an organizer, student, designer, researcher, or community member, your role in shaping this space matters. Join us as we imagine and decide what the UDesign Justice Initiative can become, grounded in care, equity, and a shared desire for transformative change.
