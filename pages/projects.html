Exploring Projects Rooted in Community and Equity

These projects are stories. They speak of resistance, reclamation, creativity, and collective care. Designed with—not for—communities, each one challenges norms and reimagines space.

By UDJ Studio April 12, 2025

Community Pavilion

Built in partnership with local residents and youth, this pavilion becomes more than a structure—it becomes a gathering space rooted in oral history and seasonal ritual. Circular forms, reclaimed materials, and a rhythm aligned with the sun’s path ground the architecture in ancestral memory.

The process was led through story circles and co-design sprints, emphasizing listening over leading. It demonstrates how design can serve as a practice of care, reciprocity, and emergence.

By CISA x DJC Collab March 4, 2025

Restorative Library

This reimagined public library shifts away from silence and restriction toward vibrancy and belonging. Created through multi-lingual engagement, it centers access, care, and storytelling.

Intergenerational planning teams helped map desires: children wanted play; elders wanted pause; teens wanted safe zones. These inputs shaped the space into layered zones of interaction and rest.

By UDJ Mobile Team January 20, 2025

Mobile Studio

Part classroom, part amplifier—the mobile studio is a traveling platform for neighborhoods to tell their own stories. It stops in underrepresented communities and offers free hands-on design justice education.

From screenprinting zines to digital storytelling, the bus facilitates imagination. It’s a rolling archive, an invitation to reclaim space, and a model for public infrastructure shaped by the public.
